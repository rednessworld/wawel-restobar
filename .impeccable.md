# Wawel Restobar — Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Barcelona locals and tourists discovering Polish cuisine, primarily Spanish and Catalan speakers with English fallback. They're making a dining decision — browsing on mobile at moderate scroll speed, likely in an "explore and decide" mindset. The job to be done: feel the atmosphere, trust the quality, and make a reservation. They should leave the page with an emotional impression, not just information.

### Brand Personality
**Warm. Authentic. Nostalgic.**

Voice: intimate and unhurried, like a letter from a grandmother in Kraków. Not formal, not casual — *personal*. The restaurant has a story and tells it with quiet confidence.

Emotional goals: warmth, belonging, quiet anticipation. The interface should feel like stepping into candlelight from a cold Barcelona street.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Reference energy:** Noma Copenhagen / Septime Paris — editorial, story-driven, cinematic stillness. Long moments between interactions. Typography does the heavy lifting. Every detail earns its place.

**Visual language already established:**
- Deep espresso (`#1C1612`) and warm parchment (`#F2E8D5`) as the structural opposites
- Amber copper (`#C8822A`) as the warmth accent — used sparingly
- Terracotta (`#A64E3A`) for price/highlight moments
- Grain noise textures on all major backgrounds — gives analogue warmth
- Cormorant Garamond in italic for emotion; DM Sans for clarity
- Polaroid gallery, flip menu cards, scroll-expansion hero — all established patterns

**Intentional dark/light rhythm:**
Dark sections (hero, menu, gallery, testimonials) create drama and atmosphere.
Warm cream sections (about, reservations, location) feel human and grounded.
This alternation is load-bearing — never flatten it.

**Hard anti-references:**
- No fast-casual energy (no loud CTAs, no discount banners, no emoji overload)
- No Polish folk art kitsch (no folk patterns, roosters, or "traditional" clip art)
- No cold modern tech aesthetic (no flat white, no stark grey, no brutal grid)
- No SaaS card layouts (no feature grids, no icon-text-icon-text rows)
- No purple gradients, ever
- No Inter font, anywhere

### Design Principles

1. **Atmosphere over information.** Every section should feel like an environment, not a data sheet. Whitespace, texture, and pacing matter as much as content.

2. **Typography carries the emotion.** Cormorant Garamond italic is the heart of the brand voice. Give headings room to breathe. Never compress or rush them.

3. **Restraint is the luxury.** Less is more. One strong accent element per section. Avoid decoration for its own sake — every visual element must earn its place.

4. **Rhythm through contrast.** The dark/warm alternation between sections is intentional and structural. Maintain it. It creates the visual breathing of the page.

5. **Accessible by default.** WCAG AA contrast on all text. Honour `prefers-reduced-motion` for all Framer Motion animations — provide static fallbacks. Never rely on colour alone to convey meaning.
